CSP Migration — Comms Plan Dashboard

Pre-Launch to Month End  |  ~1,200 CSPs  |  Owner: Shiva  |  March 2026

What Is This?

The Migration

Wiom is migrating ~1,200 CSPs (Channel Service Partners) from a legacy partner contract to a new system-governed operating model. Everything — partner state, earnings, device tracking, quality score, next actions — moves onto a new app (target: ~April 7).

All partner communication must work without human mediation — field support and BDO teams have been removed.

Three Things Changing Simultaneously

Earning StructureBonus was split across device recovery, SLA, and customer rating. Now it's a single monthly performance bonus.
Work AllocationNo fixed area, no guaranteed work. System assigns work based on performance — better performance = more work.
Interface + PayGOld app replaced by new app. Customers now take PayG plans. New app is the single source of truth for state, earnings, devices, tickets, and next actions.

The Core Risk

The risk is NOT that partners resist the change. The risk is that partners open the new app without any mental model of what has changed, see things they don't recognise, and either flood the PTL (Trust Line) with calls or disengage entirely.

Why Partners Will Actually Open the App

The #1 reason a partner opens the app daily is work — service tickets and installation requests from customers. This is the natural hook. The new system, earning info, device tracking, and education modules sit alongside the primary workflow, not as separate destinations. Every comms message that says "App kholein" is reinforced by the fact that the partner already needs the app to do their job.

Comms Objective — How We Educate the CSP

This plan is a sequenced education journey designed so that by month-end, every partner:

  • Understands what changed and why (earning, work allocation, PayG, interface)
  • Knows how to earn — rate card, bonus tiers, what helps/hurts their quality score
  • Is on the new app and using it daily for tickets + self-serve state/payouts
  • Trusts the system — no surprises, every number explained before it appears
Design principle: Every message must have intent. If there's no trigger, the message doesn't go out. WA is the nudge — the app, video, and quiz carry the real education weight.

Wiom Paathshala — Permanent Education Home

All comms content must live permanently in one place. When a dashboard card or pop-up expires, the partner should still be able to find that information. The app will have a "Wiom Paathshala" section (or equivalent) inside Settings / Help where all education modules, explainer videos, and guides are permanently accessible.

  • Rate Card — lives in a dedicated Earnings section (permanent, always accessible)
  • System Reboot video, PayG education, quiz modules — lives in Wiom Paathshala
  • DOs/DON'Ts, performance tips — lives in Wiom Paathshala
  • Device management guide — lives in Wiom Paathshala
This means every comms card in the timeline below that shows "temporary" content should also note where the content permanently lives in the app.
