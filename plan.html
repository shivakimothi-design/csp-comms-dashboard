5-Day Communication Plan — Intent-Based Triggers

05/04/26 → 10/04/26  |  Education mapped to partner's natural behaviour in the app  |  Contextual first, then Blocker

The Core Idea

Don't educate when we want to. Educate when the partner's intent is highest.

When a partner opens the Wallet — they're thinking about money. That's the moment to tell them about earning changes.
When a partner resolves a ticket — they're in work mode. That's the moment to tell them about quality score.
When a partner checks NetBox/Assets — they're thinking about devices. That's the moment to tell them about carry fee.

Instead of dumping everything on app open, we plant education in the sections partners already visit. The content matches their current mindset.

Hybrid Approach — Contextual + On-the-Face

Not everything maps to a section. Some content naturally fits a section trigger (carry fee → Asset section). Some content has no natural section home and must be told directly on the face (PayG, protection, app transition). The plan uses both.

Two mechanisms, used in combination:

MechanismWhen to useContent examples
Contextual Content maps directly to a section the partner is visiting. Their intent matches the education. Earning changes → Wallet/Earnings section
Quality score → Tickets section
Carry fee, deposit → Asset/NetBox section
On the face Content doesn't map to any section, OR is too important to wait for a section visit, OR partner may never visit the relevant section. PayG changes (no natural section)
Full overview / 3-change announcement
Protection / safety net
App transition instructions
The hybrid rule: If it can be contextual, make it contextual (higher intent = better retention). If it can't, or if it's critical and can't be left to chance, put it on the face.

Contextual First Touch + Blocker

Partner's natural behaviour triggers the first education — then blocker follows

Step 1: Contextual popup in the section they visit

All section popups are planted on 05/04. Whichever section the partner visits first — that popup fires. One-time per section.

Wallet Fires on: partner opens Wallet
Earning structure badal rahi hai.
₹300 per connection — yeh same rahega. Bonus ab monthly hoga — quality ke basis par, up to ₹120.
Puri details naye app mein milegi — jald launch ho raha hai.
Why here: Partner is in money-mode. Earning change lands when they're already thinking about income.
Tickets Fires on: partner opens/resolves a ticket
Aapka kaam ab system se aayega.
Connections system assign karega — mandatory install. Aap jitna accha kaam karenge, utna zyada kaam milega.
Quality count hogi — uptime, speed, resolution time, complaints, installation quality.
Why here: Partner is actively working. Work allocation change lands when they're doing the actual work.
Earnings Fires on: partner opens Earnings section
Earning ka naya structure aa raha hai.
₹300/connection — same. Performance bonus: up to ₹120/month.
5 cheezein matter karengi: connection uptime, speed, issue resolution, repeat complaints, installation quality.
Monthly calculate hoga. Full breakdown naye app mein.
Why here: Partner is checking their income. Earning details are exactly what they came to see.

Aapke device-wise details naye app mein dikhenge.

Fallback: If partner only visits Home and nothing else, they still get the headline. Points them to explore other sections.

Step 2: Blocker — on the NEXT app open

After a partner has seen ANY contextual popup (Step 1), their next app open triggers the blocker. Cannot skip. This is the only blocker in the entire migration.

The Blocker Content — Full Overview:

3 visual cards (swipeable, must see all):

Card 1 — Work: Connections ab system se aayengi. Mandatory install. No manual process. App zaruri hai — sab kuch yahan hoga.

Card 2 — Earning: ₹300/connection same. Bonus ab ek monthly amount — up to ₹120. Quality pe depend: uptime, speed, resolution, complaints, installation quality.

Card 3 — PayG: Customers ab apne hisaab se recharge karenge — monthly plans nahi. Daily, weekly, jitna chahein. Aapka kaam: har connection ka 30-day ISP recharge ensure karein.

Final screen: "Samajh gaya" — must tap to dismiss.
Why contextual BEFORE blocker?
If the blocker fires cold, they're irritated — they came to do work. They'll speed-tap through.

But if they've already seen a contextual popup ("earning badal rahi hai" while checking wallet), the blocker is warm. They have a piece of the puzzle. The blocker gives the full picture — they engage because they know it's relevant.

Why PayG is in the blocker, not contextual:
PayG has no natural section in the old app. There's no "customer plans" section the partner visits. It must be told directly on the face. The blocker is the right place — it covers all 3 changes including the one with no section home.

Remaining Section Popups Fire Naturally

As partner navigates to sections they haven't visited yet, they see the relevant education

No new content is pushed. The popups planted on Day 1 are still waiting in sections the partner hasn't visited yet. As they naturally navigate:

  • Partner visits Wallet (if not seen on Day 1) → earning popup fires
  • Partner visits Tickets → work/quality popup fires
  • Partner visits Asset/NetBox → device economics popup fires
  • Partner visits Earnings → full earning breakdown popup fires
Each popup fires once per section. Once seen, it's gone. Partner can revisit the content in Wiom Paathshala anytime.

No WA or SMS on these days. The partner is discovering education through their natural app usage. Pushing them externally would break the organic flow.
Fallback: What if a partner doesn't visit certain sections?
By end of Day 3, if a partner has NOT seen the blocker (didn't open app at all since Day 1), we send a WA nudge: "Wiom app mein zaruri update hai — kholein aur dekhein." This is the only forced outreach on Days 2-3.

07/04

Protection Confirmation — You Have Time

After 2 days of change education, the reassurance message

Mechanism: WA text (this one is short enough for WA) + contextual popup on next app open.

Content:

  • New system is launching on 10/04
  • No performance enforcement until 01/05/26
  • This is time to understand and adjust
  • Trust Line available for any doubts: [number]
Why Day 3: Days 1-2 = "what changed" (anxiety builds). Day 3 = "you have time" (anxiety relief). The protection message has maximum impact when the partner has already absorbed the changes and is worried.

08/04

Video + Quiz — Deep Education

For partners who want full understanding — video surfaces in Paathshala + prompted via popup

Mechanism: Contextual popup on app open — "Pura system detail mein samjhein — video dekhein aur quiz karein." Links to Wiom Paathshala where video + quiz lives.

Video content (2-3 min):

  • Work: System assigns connections, mandatory, performance-based allocation, app is essential
  • Earning: ₹300/connection, bonus up to ₹120, 5 quality factors, NetBox economics (₹2/day, ₹50, ₹200 deposit)
  • PayG: Customers recharge as needed, not monthly. Your job: 30-day ISP recharge.

Quiz (3-4 questions): Same format that worked for PayG earlier. Tests comprehension. Must pass.

Why Day 4 and not Day 1: By Day 4, the partner has already encountered 2-3 contextual popups across different sections + the blocker. They have the headline and pieces. The video now connects everything into one coherent story. It's not new information — it's the full picture of what they've been seeing in fragments.

Why not force it: A 2-3 min video as a blocker on Day 1 = partner taps through without watching. By Day 4, they're curious (they've seen the pieces) and the video satisfies that curiosity.
WA nudge (same day): "Wiom ka pura naya system ek video mein samjhein — app mein Wiom Paathshala kholein, quiz bhi karein." Redirect only.

09/04

Get the New App — Transition Day

Practical instruction: download/update before tomorrow's launch

Mechanism: Contextual popup on app open + WA + SMS (triple channel — cannot be missed).

Content:

  • New app launches tomorrow (10/04)
  • Download new app / update current app: [Play Store link]
  • Old app will stop working from tomorrow
  • Your tickets, earnings, devices — everything will be in the new app
Why Day 5: Not earlier ("download and wait" = bad experience). Not launch day (too late, partner needs app ready). Day before = partner downloads overnight, app is ready for 10/04 morning.
🚀
10/04

Launch — Declaration Becomes Reality

New app live. Old app blocked. Partner sees their individual numbers for the first time.
  • Old app: Blocked. Redirect screen: "Yeh app band hai. Naya Wiom app kholein."
  • WA + SMS: "Naya app live hai" + deep link + Trust Line number
  • New app first open: Popup with CSP-specific data — their rate card, their device status, how the app works
  • After popup → dashboard with tickets on top (their daily work driver)
What was accomplished in 5 days:

Every partner who opens the new app on 10/04:
✔ Has seen at least 1-2 contextual popups relevant to their app usage
✔ Has gone through the blocker (full 3-change overview)
✔ Has had access to video + quiz in Paathshala
✔ Knows they have protection until 01/05
✔ Has the new app installed

When they see individual numbers (their devices, their score, their bonus projection), nothing is a surprise. The rules were already explained — now they see their own data against those rules.
