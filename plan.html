5-Day Communication Plan

05/04/26 (Announcement) → 10/04/26 (App Live)  |  Old App: Popup + Static Only  |  1 Blocker, Rest Contextual

The Setup

We have 5 days (05/04 to 09/04) to educate 1,200 CSPs on the old app before the new app goes live on 10/04.

Two mechanisms available:

  1. On the face (Blocker) — forced popup, partner cannot do anything until they acknowledge. Used once.
  2. Contextual — popup triggered when partner performs an action (opens app, clicks ticket, visits homepage). Shows once per content piece, then gone. Dismissable.
Old app constraint: Static content only. Same content for all 1,200 CSPs. No individual device counts, no bonus history, no personalised state. Declaration mode only.

Content to communicate across 5 days:

  1. Work now comes from system (mandatory connections, app essential)
  2. Earning structure (₹300 stays, ₹120 bonus quality-dependent, 5 quality factors)
  3. NetBox economics (₹2/day idle fee, ₹50 collection, ₹200 security deposit)
  4. PayG (customers recharge as needed, not monthly. CSP ensures 30-day ISP recharge)
  5. Protection (no enforcement until 01/05, time to adjust)
  6. App transition (download/update new app)

Day-by-Day Plan

1
05/04

The Announcement — 3 Things Are Changing

The one and only blocker in the entire migration
Blocker — must acknowledge Old App popup WA redirect SMS redirect
Mechanism

On the face (Blocker). Partner opens old app → full-screen popup. Cannot dismiss, cannot access tickets, cannot do anything until they acknowledge. This is the only time we do this.

Trigger

App open. Fires immediately. No action needed from partner to trigger it.

Content — 3 visual cards (swipeable)
  • Card 1 — Work: Connections now come from the system. Mandatory to install. No manual process. App is where all work happens now.
  • Card 2 — Earning: ₹300 per connection stays the same. Bonus is now one monthly amount (up to ₹120) — depends on quality of your work. Details coming tomorrow.
  • Card 3 — PayG: Customers ab apne hisaab se recharge karenge. Monthly plans nahi — daily, weekly, jitna chahein. Your job: ensure 30-day ISP recharge for every connection.

Final screen: "Samajh gaya / समझ गया" acknowledge button.

WA + SMS (same day)

Redirect message: "Wiom app kholein — zaruri update hai." Does not contain the education — just gets them to open the old app where the blocker is waiting.

Why this scope — not more, not less
Why not the full earning breakdown? Information overload. Day 1 is about the FACT that 3 things changed — not the details of each. The ₹120 bonus factors, carry fee specifics, NetBox economics all come later when the partner has absorbed the headline.

Why not just earning? If we only tell them earning changed, they'll panic about money without knowing the full picture (work allocation, PayG). All three must land together so the partner sees the complete shape of the change.

Why blocker? This is the one moment where we force attention. If this lands as a dismissable popup, a partner who's rushing to complete a ticket will skip it — and then open the new app on 10/04 completely blind. That's the exact failure mode this plan exists to prevent.
What this does NOT contain
No specific numbers beyond ₹300 and "up to ₹120." The 5 quality factors, carry fee rate, security deposit — all come on subsequent days.

No "download new app" — app isn't ready for 5 more days. Premature instruction.

No protection promise — partner hasn't felt the anxiety yet. Protection is meaningful after they've absorbed the details, not before.

06/04

Deep Dive — Video + Quiz

How the new system actually works — earning, work allocation, PayG in detail
Contextual — on app open Old App popup Video (2-3 min) Quiz gate WA redirect
Mechanism

Contextual. Partner opens app → popup offers "Kal ka update samjha? Ab detail mein dekhein." Dismissable — but video + quiz is the path. If dismissed, resurfaces next app open (max 2 attempts, then stops).

Trigger

First app open on 06/04. If dismissed, retriggers on next app open (once more). After that, available in-app voluntarily.

Content — Video (2-3 min)
  • Work Allocation (detailed):
    • Connections come from system — mandatory to install
    • No manual process — everything through the app
    • Better performance = more work. Lower = less.
    • App is where tickets, installations, service requests all happen
  • Earning (detailed):
    • ₹300 per connection — unchanged
    • Bonus up to ₹120/month — depends on 5 quality factors:
        1. Connection uptime (most important)
        2. Speed / performance quality
        3. Issue resolution timeliness
        4. Repeat issues / complaints
        5. Installation quality
  • PayG (detailed):
    • Customers recharge as per their need — NOT monthly plans
    • Daily, weekly — whatever they want
    • Your responsibility: ensure 30-day ISP recharge for all connections
Quiz (follows video)

3-4 questions testing comprehension. Same format that worked for PayG education earlier. Partner must pass to complete the module. Example questions:

  • Q: Ab bonus kaise calculate hoga? → A: One monthly performance bonus
  • Q: Customer ab kaise recharge karega? → A: Apne hisaab se, monthly plan nahi
  • Q: Zyada kaam kaise milega? → A: Better performance = system assigns more work
Why video + quiz, not just text
Yesterday was the headline. Today is the explanation. Three simultaneous changes (work + earning + PayG) cannot be explained in a text popup. A 2-3 minute video with visual anchors is re-watchable, shareable among partners, and lands concepts that text cannot.

Quiz ensures comprehension. A video can be played and ignored. A quiz forces engagement with the content. The PayG quiz format worked — we're reusing what worked.
What this does NOT contain
No NetBox economics. Carry fee, security deposit, collection — these are financial details that should land separately (Day 3). Including them here overloads the video with too many money topics.

No protection promise. Partner hasn't finished absorbing the details yet. Protection is reassurance — it's more powerful after the full picture is clear.
WA (same day)
"Kal ki update ke baad — ab detail mein samjhein. Wiom app kholein, video dekhein aur quiz complete karein."

Does not contain the education. Redirect only.

07/04

NetBox Economics — Carry Fee, Collection, Security Deposit

The money specifics that directly affect devices they hold
Contextual — on app open Old App popup
Mechanism

Contextual. Popup on first app open. Dismissable. Shows once.

Trigger

First app open on 07/04.

Content
  • Carry Fee: Any NetBox idle for 15+ days → ₹2/day charge. Your device-wise details will be visible in the new app when it launches.
  • Device Collection: Returning a NetBox to Wiom → you get ₹50 per device.
  • Security Deposit:
    • When you receive a device: ₹200 security deposit is held
    • When you return properly: ₹200 returned in full
    • If not returned or damaged: ₹200 adjusted against device liability
    • Your money — held temporarily. Ensures proper handling.
Why today — not earlier, not later
Day 1 said earning changed. Day 2 explained how bonus works. Today adds the device-specific financial policies that sit alongside the bonus.

Showing this on Day 1 or 2 would have overloaded the partner with too many money points at once. By Day 3, the earning structure is absorbed — now they can process these specifics.

Why not on Day 4 or 5? Because the new app (Day 6) will show individual device counts and carry fee accruing. If the partner first sees "₹2/day" inside the new app without prior context, they call Trust Line. This must land before they see the numbers.
What this does NOT contain
No individual device counts. Old app can't show CSP-specific data. We say "your device details will be in the new app" — not "you have 5 devices and 2 are idle." That's Phase 2 (new app).
4
08/04

Protection — You Have Time to Adjust

The safety net after 3 days of change announcements
Contextual — on app open Old App popup WA text
Mechanism

Contextual. Popup on first app open. Dismissable. Also sent as WA text (standalone — not a redirect, this one is short enough for WA).

Trigger

First app open on 08/04 + WA text delivery same day.

Content
  • The new system is launching on 10/04
  • Initially, focus is on understanding and setup — not enforcement
  • No performance enforcement until 01/05/26
  • You'll have time to understand, adjust, and ask questions
  • Trust Line available for any doubts: [number]
Why today — the psychology
Day 1: "Things are changing" → anxiety starts.
Day 2: "Here's how it works in detail" → anxiety peaks (the details make it real).
Day 3: "Here's the money impact on your devices" → anxiety is high.
Day 4: "You have time. No enforcement until May." → relief.

This sequencing is deliberate. The protection message has maximum impact when the partner has already absorbed the changes and is worried. If we said "don't worry" on Day 1 before they knew what to worry about, it would mean nothing.
Why WA is OK here
This is the one message short enough for WA. It's not education — it's reassurance. "No enforcement until May 1. Time to adjust." A partner reads this on WA and feels better. They don't need to open the app for this to work (though the popup reinforces it).

09/04

Get the New App — Transition Instructions

Practical step: download/update the new app before tomorrow's launch
Contextual — on app open Old App popup WA + deep link SMS + deep link
Mechanism

Contextual. Popup on app open. Also WA + SMS with Play Store deep link. Triple-channel because this must not be missed — no app = no access tomorrow.

Trigger

First app open on 09/04 + WA + SMS delivery same day.

Content
  • New app launches tomorrow (10/04)
  • Download the new app now: [Play Store link]
  • [If new app]: Delete the old Wiom app after downloading new one
  • [If update]: Update your current Wiom app from the link
  • Tomorrow, your tickets, earnings, devices — everything will be in the new app
  • Old app will stop working from tomorrow
Why today — not earlier, not on launch day
Not earlier: "Download an app you can't use yet" creates a bad experience. The partner downloads, opens it, sees nothing useful, and loses trust. The download should happen the day before launch so the app is ready when it goes live.

Not on launch day: If we wait until 10/04 to tell partners to download, half of them are trying to work (tickets) and can't access the new app yet. The download + install takes time. Getting it done the night before means 10/04 morning is smooth.

10/04

Launch Day — New App Live, Old App Blocked

Declaration becomes reality. Partner sees their individual numbers for the first time.
New App live Old App blocked WA + deep link SMS backup
What happens
  • Old app: Blocked. Any partner who opens it sees only a redirect screen — "This app is closed. Open the new Wiom app." No functionality remains.
  • WA + SMS: "Naya Wiom app live hai" with deep link. Trust Line number.
  • New app first open: Popup/overlay with CSP-specific data:
    • Their rate card (earning structure — now tied to their account)
    • Their device status (active vs idle — first time seeing real counts)
    • How the app works (tickets, earnings, devices all here)
  • After popup dismissed → regular dashboard with tickets on top
The shift
Phase 1 said: "Idle devices will cost ₹2/day."
Phase 2 shows: "You have 2 idle devices. ₹2/day is accruing."

Same rule, now personal. This is why we didn't show device counts earlier — old app couldn't, and showing wrong numbers destroys trust faster than showing no numbers.
What was accomplished in 5 days
By 10/04, the partner who opens the new app:

✔ Knows that 3 things changed (Day 1)
✔ Understands how earning, work, and PayG work (Day 2)
✔ Knows the device financial policies (Day 3)
✔ Knows they have until 01/05 to adjust (Day 4)
✔ Has the new app installed (Day 5)

When they see their individual numbers in the new app, nothing is a surprise. The rules were already explained — now they just see their own data against those rules.

This is the difference between a Trust Line flood and a smooth transition.
